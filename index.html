The Deck of Hard Truths

An interactive cybersecurity roundtable game
CIO CISO XGermany Munich 2026

What is this?

A deck of 26 cards designed to spark real conversations between security leaders. No vendor pitches. No textbook answers. Just hard truths.

Draw a card. React. The table votes. Repeat.

The Three Card Types

🔴
Breach Scenario
8 cards
"This just happened. What do you do?"
You draw a real-world breach scenario. You have 60 seconds to describe how you'd respond. Then the table votes: Contained or Catastrophic?
🟡
Hot Take
10 cards
"Defend this. 30 seconds. Even if you disagree."
You draw a provocative statement. You must defend it for 30 seconds - even if you personally disagree. Then the table debates. No sitting on the fence.
🔵
Trade-Off
8 cards
"Pick a side. No 'it depends.'"
Two competing priorities. You must pick one and defend your choice. "Both" and "it depends" are not allowed. The table shows hands for each side.

How to Play

  1. Fan the cards face-down
    Place all cards in the center of the table. Color-coded backs are visible so you know the type before you draw.
  2. Draw a card
    The moderator picks someone or asks for volunteers. You can choose your color (risk level) or go blind.
  3. Read it aloud
    The moderator reads the card to the table so the drawer has a moment to think.
  4. Respond
    🔴 60 seconds for breach scenarios. 🟡 30 seconds for hot takes. 🔵 Pick a side immediately for trade-offs.
  5. Table reacts
    Show of hands, quick debate, or the moderator picks a dissenter to challenge. Keep it to 2-3 minutes total per card.
  6. Move on
    Don't overanalyze. The energy is in the snap decisions, not the deep dives. That's what the main discussion is for.

Ground Rules

✅ Do

  • Be honest. "I don't know" is a valid answer.
  • Challenge each other. Disagreement is the point.
  • Share real stories. Anonymize if needed, but keep it real.
  • Have fun. This is serious content, not a serious vibe.

🚫 Don't

  • Give vendor pitches. We're all here as practitioners.
  • Say "it depends" on trade-offs. That's cheating.
  • Dominate the table. One card, one response, then share.
  • Take it outside the room. Chatham House Rule applies.

Pro Tips

For the Moderator

Read the card aloud yourself so the drawer can process. Push back on corporate answers: "OK, but what would you ACTUALLY do at 3 AM?" After each response, quick table poll: "Who agrees? Who'd do something completely different?"

For Participants

The best answers are the honest ones, not the polished ones. If your gut reaction is "we'd be screwed" - say that. Everyone at this table has been there.

For Everyone

These cards work at any security roundtable, team offsite, or leadership meeting. Steal the format. Make your own cards. The goal is better conversations, not a perfect game.
